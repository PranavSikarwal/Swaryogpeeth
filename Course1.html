Our residential program of Yoga Teacher Training in Rishikesh is held at our peaceful yoga and meditation centre, surrounded by nature and providing a serene and harmonious environment for your learning and personal growth. At Swar Yog Peeth, we are dedicated to supporting your journey towards becoming a confident and knowledgeable yoga instructor. Curriculum

A 200-hour yoga teacher training course is a comprehensive program that covers all the essential aspects of yoga. The curriculum is designed to provide students with a thorough understanding of the practice and principles of yoga, as well as the skills and knowledge needed to become a competent and confident yoga teacher. Here are the details of the curriculum:


  • Asana (yoga postures): Students will learn the proper alignment, modifications, and variations of various yoga postures, including standing, seated, inversions, backbends, twists, and forward bends. This will include the study of Sun Salutations, standing and seated postures, inversions, backbends, twists, forward bends, and restorative postures. Students will also learn how to sequence
  • Pranayama (breathing exercises): Students will learn various breathing techniques to enhance their yoga practice and promote relaxation, including ujjayi, nadi shodhana, and bhastrika. Students will learn how to incorporate pranayama in their yoga classes and how to use it for different purposes, such as relaxation, energizing or balancing.
  • Meditation: Students will learn different methods of meditation, including concentration, mindfulness, and visualization, and how to incorporate it into their personal practice and teaching. Students will learn different techniques to help the students to focus and to cultivate inner awareness.
  • Yoga Philosophy and History: Students will learn about the history, philosophy, and ethics of yoga, including the Yoga Sutras of Patanjali and other key texts, such as Bhagavad Gita, Hatha Yoga Pradipika, and Upanishads. This includes the study of different yoga traditions, such as Hatha Yoga, Raja Yoga, and Bhakti Yoga, and the study of the main concepts of yoga, such as the chakras, koshas, and the 8 limbs of yoga.
  • Anatomy and Physiology: Students will learn the basics of human anatomy and physiology, including how yoga postures affect the body, how to adapt yoga for different populations, and how to recognize and work with common injuries. This includes the study of the bones, muscles, and joints of the body, and how they are affected by different postures.
  • Teaching Methodology: Students will learn how to effectively teach yoga, including how to create and lead safe and effective yoga classes, give clear and effective verbal and hands-on adjustments, and create a positive and supportive learning environment. This includes the study of different teaching styles, how to plan and structure a class, how to use language effectively, and how to create a class that is inclusive and accessible to all.
  • Sequencing and Theming: Students will learn how to create thoughtful and dynamic yoga sequences, and how to theme classes to target specific areas of the body or to address specific needs. This includes the study of how to create a balanced class, how to use different themes to create a specific atmosphere and how to create sequences that are appropriate for different levels of students.
  • Practicum: Students will have the opportunity to practice teaching yoga under the guidance of experienced teachers, and receive feedback and guidance on their teaching skills. This includes the opportunity to observe experienced teachers and to teach classes to peers and to the public.
  • Self-study and reflection: Students will be encouraged to engage in self-study, reflection, and journaling as part of their training. This includes the opportunity to reflect on the learning experience, to deepen the understanding of the material studied and to integrate the new knowledge in a personal practice.
  • Ethical considerations: Students will learn about the ethical considerations that they should be aware of as a yoga teacher, including boundaries, cultural sensitivity, and inclusivity. This includes the study of how to create a safe and respectful learning environment, how to address sensitive issues and how to create classes that are inclusive and accessible to all students regardless of race, gender, sexual orientation, age, religion and physical abilities.


Additional (covered partially in 200 hours YTTC) :



  • Sanskrit and Mantra:Students will learn the basics of the Sanskrit language, the language of ancient yoga texts, and the meaning and use of common yoga terms and mantras. This includes the study of the basics of the Sanskrit alphabet, common yoga terms, and the meaning and use of mantras in yoga practice and teaching.
  • Ayurveda and Yoga: Students will learn about the principles of Ayurveda and how to integrate them into their yoga practice and teaching. This includes the study of the three doshas (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha), how to use Ayurvedic principles to create a balanced diet and lifestyle, and how to use Ayurvedic principles to create yoga classes that balance the doshas.
  • Yoga for specific purposes: Students will learn how to use yoga for specific purposes, such as yoga for athletes, yoga for stress management, yoga for sleep, and yoga for mental health. This includes the study of how to create classes for specific purposes and how to use different techniques to achieve specific goals, such as yoga for stress management, yoga for sleep, and yoga for mental health.
  • Special Populations: Students will learn how to adapt yoga for special populations, including individuals with injuries or special needs. This includes the study of how to modify postures for injuries and conditions, how to create classes for specific populations such as seniors, children, pregnant women, and people with chronic illnesses, and how to create classes for specific goals such as weight loss, stress reduction, and flexibility.
  • Marketing and Business: Students will learn how to market themselves as a yoga teacher, how to create a yoga business plan, how to set up and manage a yoga studio, and how to use social media and other marketing tools to promote their business. This includes the study of how to create a brand, how to set prices, how to create a website, how to use social media, how to create a newsletter, how to create a brochure, and how to create a business card.

Attending each class is essential to your growth as a student. You will not only acquire knowledge from your teachers and textbooks, but also from your daily interactions and experiences in school. It’s important to note that all classes are mandatory, unless there is a legitimate emergency or illness that prevents you from attending. Course Prerequisites

  1. level of yoga experience: A minimum of 3 months of consistent yoga practice is recommended prior to the course, to ensure that students are comfortable with basic yoga postures and can fully benefit from the training. It is useful to have participated in Ashtanga or Vinyasa Flow styles of yoga before joining the program, as they are dynamic and fluid forms of yoga. If you’ve been involved with a closely related fitness or sports training background, this may also be an advantage to you, but this is not essential. While it is not essential to have years of experience on the mat, signing up with an open heart and an open mind are the best two things you can bring with you.
  2. Physical requirements: The program can be physically demanding at times, so students should be in good physical health and able to perform the physical requirements of the program. This includes being able to stand for long periods, holding yoga postures and practicing yoga sequences.
  3. English proficiency: As the program is conducted in English, students should have a basic level of English proficiency to understand and participate in the program. This is important for being able to fully understand and participate in the lectures and discussions.
  4. Age requirement: Some schools may have a minimum age requirement, usually 18 years old. Yoga is a practice for anyone and everyone to enjoy regardless of their age, experience, or background. Emotional maturity is something required in all students during a course, so it’s about undertaking a training at the right time for you, But we accept students above 16 years of age only.
  5. Health conditions: If a student has any specific health conditions, they should consult with us before applying, to ensure that the program is suitable for them. This includes any physical, mental or emotional conditions that may prevent the student from fully participating in the program.

Trainee Assessment

To become a certified Yoga teacher, you will be evaluated on several factors throughout your training program. These include your class participation, written assessments, and ongoing observations by senior teachers. Your teaching skills will also be assessed through practical exercises and you will receive valuable feedback from both senior and assistant teachers.

To meet the required standards for graduation, you will need to demonstrate a solid understanding of anatomy, asana alignment, adjustments, and effective communication. You will work with other trainees on teaching practices, so it is important to approach this part of the training with maturity and respect for your peers and teachers. You can expect to receive constructive coaching and feedback throughout the program to help you grow and develop as a Yoga teacher.

To support your studies, you will receive a comprehensive manual from Swar Yog Peeth, as well as various study materials and articles. This manual is not only a valuable resource during the training, but will also serve as a reference after graduation. So, get ready to roll up your sleeves and immerse yourself in the exciting world of Yoga!
